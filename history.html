Introduction

Madhubani painting is a traditional Indian art form practiced in the Mithila region of Bihar, India. Traditionally, it is done by women on walls and floors during special occasions and festivals.

Historical Background

This art form dates back to the era of the Ramayana when King Janaka commissioned artists to paint scenes of his daughter Sitaâ€™s wedding. The tradition has been passed down for generations.

Timeline & Development

  • Ancient Times: Painted on mud walls of buildings and temples.
  • 20th Century: Shifted to paper and cloth for preservation.
  • 1960s: Promoted by NGOs and artists like Sita Devi, Ganga Devi.
  • Present Day: Exhibited in global art galleries.

Styles of Madhubani Painting

  • Bharni: Filled with bright colors, often of deities and goddesses.
  • Kachni: Line drawings with minimal color.
  • Tantrik: Based on religious and tantric symbolism.
  • Godna: Tattoo-style geometric patterns.
  • Kohbar: Used in wedding ceremonies.
